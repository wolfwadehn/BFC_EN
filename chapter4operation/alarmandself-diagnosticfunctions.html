Alarm and self-diagnostic functions

6.1 Alarm display

CypCutE monitors all components when the machine is operating. Once an alarm is detected, the processing will be stopped and the alarm message is displayed on the title bar in red background color. Motion functions will be disabled until the alarm is cleared. Check the source of the alarm, clear the alarm and then resume processing. The alarm title is shown as below:

Figure 1. Figure 4- 20

The “Alarm” window at the screen bottom will also display the alarm message. After the alarm source is cleared, the alarm title will disappear but the alarm message is recorded at the alarm window. Double click “System” window to check all history records and software events.

In addition to alarm messages, CypCutE will display warning, notice and important messages in different colors. These messages will not stop the machining but attention to these messages and related preventive measures are still recommended.

Alarms can be reset or manually cleared. Go to “Process” and “Global parameters” to check all the alarms to be manually cleared .

6.2 Self-diagnostic function

During processing, click the “” button in File menu on the title bar. Select “Control card monitoring”, and the status information of all parts can be checked to determine if there is any problem throughout the cutting process.

Figure 2. Figure 4- 21

Signals of axes and feedback values of the encoder are displayed in “Axis”. “Pulse Test” is used to send fixed pulse to the homo-axis and check the accuracy of pulse equivalent. “Limit Signal” is used to show if the cutting head reaches the limit positions. PWM indicates the machine on/off status and the effectiveness of 15 input ports and 20 output ports.

“Encoder detection” is used to check if the encoder feedback is corresponding to the rotating direction of the motor. Software functions such as micro-segment, scan cutting, edge finding and one-key cutting-off etc. are only enabled when the encoder feedback is corresponding to the rotating direction of the motor.

6.3 Restrictions once alarms are activated

Disable servo when emergency stop alarm is activated (to prevent accidental motion).
Disable X/Y motion functions when BCS100 alarm is detected (Z axis condition is unknown under this status, but there might be collision if X/Y axis moves).
Disable Z axis motion when emergency stop alarm is activated (in complete stop status).
Disable dry run and jog when Z-axis lower limit alarm is detected (to prevent collision as in case 2 stated above ).
If the height controller alarm is detected, the “Allow axial motion” button must be pressed manually to move the axes.
