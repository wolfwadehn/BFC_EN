7.1 Machine alarm

CypCutE monitors all components when the machine is operating. Once an alarm is detected, it will be displayed on title bar in a red background and actions will be taken, e.g. the machine will be stopped. Motion operations will be disabled until the alarm is cleared. Check the machine and operate only after the warning and error messages are cleared. An example of alarm is shown as below:

Figure 1. Figure 4- 22

Besides the title bar, the “Alarm” window at the screen bottom will also display the alarm message. After the alarm source is cleared, the alarm title will disappear but alarm message in alarm window will be saved. Double click “System” window to check all history records and software events.

In addition to alarm messages, when other abnormal conditions are detected by CypCutE, they will be displayed in “System” window in different colors depending on the level of severity including warning, notice and message. These messages will not stop machine operation but attention to various messages are still recommended so that necessary measures can be taken as soon as possible.

Alarms can be reset or cleared manually. Go to “Process”- “Global parameters” to check all the alarms that need to be cleared manually.

Below are restrictions once alarms are activated:

Disable servo when emergency stop alarm is activated (to prevent accidental motion)
Disable X/Y motion functions when BCS100 alarm is detected (Z axis condition is unknown under this status, but there might be collision if X/Y axis moves)
Disable Z axis motion when emergency stop alarm is activated (in complete stop status).
Disable dry run and jog when Z-axis lower limit alarm is detected (to prevent collision as stated above ).
If the height controller alarm is detected, the“Allow axis motion” button must be pressed manually to move the axes.

Use instruction: (Required software version: CypCut V731 and above)

1. Go to “Platform configuration tool” -“Warning configuration”, tick the options “Axis Movement is Allowed after Confirming Height Controller Alarm” and “All Alarms shall be Cleared manually” and save.

Figure 2. Figure 4- 23

2. When the height controller alarm is detected, “Allow axis movement” and “Alarm reset” buttons will appear on the bottom right corner. The machine can only be operated to move after “Allow axis movement”button is pressed.

Figure 3. Figure 4- 24

3.2 Laser alarm and warning

When an alarm signal of the laser device is triggered, open the laser monitoring software to check the details of the alarm or warning message.

If an alarm is detected from the core modules of the laser device, the laser device will be switched off and the electrical system of the laser device wil be tripped. At the same time, the red LED (fault) indicator will keep flashing.

Causes of the alarms are:

Optical faults
High back-reflection is detected.
Over-temperature of internal components of the laser device.
Core modules of the laser device are not in switch-off status when switch cabinet electrical system detects abnormal conditions or the safety circuit is interrupted.

In case of any optical fault, the laser device will enter the safe mode. In this mode, the laser device is operated under restrictions with a set of pre-configured parameters. The laser device outputs low energy and low-frequency pulses to allow troubleshooting without causing further damage. Troubleshooting of optical faults requires high sensitivity, fast speed and minimal damage to the laser device. However, there is possibility that the optical monitoring system may go wrong and report a false optical fault. In this case, the safe mode can be turned off with the safe mode reset program.

Warning

The laser device will trigger warnings in certain circumstances. Currently, the red LED will be illuminated but the laser device will not be switched off. When the source of the warning is cleared, the laser device will reset the warning automatically.

Warning conditions are

Back reflection is close to the warning limit.
Temperature is closed to warning limit
Laser device is in safe mode.
Electrical system is not reset.
The laser head is not inserted into the machining head.
