Laser classes

Laser products are divided into laser classes as per the international standard IEC 60825-1(USA: ANSI Z136.1, ANSI B11.21). The laser class corresponds to the hazard level of the laser light emitted.

The machine is a class 1 laser product. When all guards are active, the accessible laser radiation from all beam sources installed is not dangerous under reasonably foreseeable conditions.

All beam sources which the personnel can come into contact with are listed below.

The section "Danger due to laser radiation" describes the situations in which personnel may come into contact with a laser source.

In addition, other beam sources can be installed which emit laser radiation (e.g., laser light barriers for component and container query). However, the personnel are not exposed to this radiation at any time during operation of the machine or during setting and maintenance work.

Laser class Description Beam source on the machine

1

The accessible laser radiation is not dangerous under normal, - foreseeable conditions.

1M

Available laser rays in the wavelength range from 302.5 nm to 400 nm. As long as the cross section of the laser rays has not been reduced by an optical instrument (magnifying glass, lens, beam expander), the available laser rays will not harm the eyes.

Available laser rays in the wavelength range from 400nm to 700nm in the visible spectrum. Short irradiation (less than 0.25s) will not harm the eyes. Other rays outside of 400-700nm meet the condition of Class 1.

Generally, the laser radiation is escaped by avoiding or closing your eyes.

-TruFiber positioning laser diode (wavelength: 630-680 nm)

2M

The accessible laser radiation ranges from 400 nm to 700 nm in the visible spectrum. As long as the laser beam is not observed through an optical instrument (magnifying glass, lens, telescope), the short irradiation (less than 0.25s) will not harm the eyes. Other rays outside the wavelength range of 400-700 nm meet the condition of Class 1M.

Generally, the laser radiation is escaped by avoiding or closing your eyes.

3R

Available laser rays in the wavelength range from 302.5 nm to 106 nm and are dangerous to eyes. The maximum power or energy is five times the limit of the permissible Class 2 laser radiation in the wavelength range of 400 nm to 700 nm.

3B

Available laser rays are harmful to the eyes and often to the skin. Looking directly at the laser beam of Class 3B laser is dangerous.

Available laser radiation of Class 3B laser equipment in excess of the maximum allowable radiation value will cause skin damage.
