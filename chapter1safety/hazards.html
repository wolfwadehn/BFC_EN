Hazards

Hazards

The "Hazards" section is based on the manufacturer’s risk assessment and describes the residual risk. The user must take measures to prevent the hazards.

6.1 Mechanical dangers

Falling or tilting machine component

The machine components must be unloaded by the user and transported to the installation site (if not otherwise agreed on in the contract).

Risk of fatal injury from falling and tilting

machine component!

Carry out transport work in accordance with the transport regulations.
Assign qualified personnel for transportation.
Observe the safety regulations for transport heavy loads.
All persons must be clear of the danger zone during machine transport.
Use certified lifting power and means of transportation of sufficient size.
Only lift components at the securing points intended for this purpose.
Only transport the machine with the transport devices mounted.
Observe center of gravity position.

Slippery surface

For maintenance or setup, it is necessary to step on individual assemblies. Surfaces of machine components may be dirty.

Slippery tread may cause fall danger!

Wear personal protective articles.
Clean the tread before entering

Slipping sheet

If the loaded sheet metal is too large or not properly aligned on the workbench, or the interception mechanism is damaged, the sheet can be pushed outwards by the pallet that is moving out when a pallet change takes place.

Risk of crushing, shearing and impact due

to slipping sheets!

Align the sheet with the stop edge of the pallet changer.
Observe the specifications for the maximum permissible sheet sizes.
Do not commission a pallet changer with a damaged interception mechanism.

Unexpected movement of pneumatic operated components

Malfunctions, mechanical defects, and stuck workpieces can lead to pneumatically operated components not reaching their required position. In these cases, energy is stored in the components, and is suddenly released when the jam is freed.

Risk of injury due to unexpected movement

of components!

Operate the manual functions for opening and closing or moving pneumatic component several times.
If the jam cannot be released using manual functions: press EMERGENCY STOP to switch off the compressed air
Wait until you hear the pneumatic devices depressurize.
Do not free stuck components and workpieces with your hands.
Maintain sufficient distance.
Release stuck or stuck workpiece with a suitable tool (e.g., rubber hammer, pliers)

Non-professional personnel or untrained personnel

shall not adjust or maintainPneumatic element

Sharp-edge workpieces

There is a risk of injury during manual loading with sheets and during manual part removal.

Workpieces have sharp edges!

Cutting injuries of the hands.

Wear protective gloves.

Travel motion in the unprotected danger zone

For the machine with automation, the monitoring of the danger zone around the machine pallet changer is integrated into the safety circuit of the automation system. In case that the automation system is switched off due to errors occurred in the automation installation area, the safeguarding of the danger zone around the machine pallet changer will be deactivated. In this situation, make sure nobody is within the danger zone around the pallet changer before operating the pallet changer.

Risk of impact and crushing due to travel

motions in the unprotected danger zone.

Observe the one-man operation rule.
Before pressing the enable key, make sure that no one is inside the danger zone,
Immediately release enable key as long as someone enters the danger zone.

Danger due to stepping on the slag cart

Danger of tripping when stepping on the

slag cart.

Do not stepping on the slag cart.
Before entering the working area, pull out the slag cart.

Danger due to stepping on the pallet

Risk of injury from stepping on the pallets!

Do not climb or walk on pallets.

Always unload pallets outside the machine using suitable

additional equipment

Before operating the pallet changer, make sure that on one is in the nearby of the pallet changer.

6.2 Dangers from electricity

Work on electrical equipment and live parts is only allowed to be carried out by trained qualified personnel

Live components

The machine is powered centrally.

However, when necessary, the machine components (laser, compact dust extractor, process cooler can be supplied with power separately. These components are not switched off by the main switch of the machine.

Machine components are live!

Risk of fatal injury due to electric shock!

Only professionals are allowed to work on electrical equipment.
Before working on components: switch off the MAIN SWWITCH and secure against switching on again.
Before touching: check parts to make sure they are deenergized.

If the electrical cabinet door is opened,

there is a dangerous voltage in all of the

electrical cabinet’s electrical components!

Risk of fatal injury due to electric shock!

Observe the warning signs.
Only give the DOOR-LOCK-DEACTIVATION key switch to authorized personnel.

Components that are still live after the MAIN SWITCH has been switched off are marked with a warning sign.

Dangerous residual voltage on components in the electrical cabinet!

Risk of fatal injury due to electric shock!

Only have qualified personnel carry out work on electrical equipment.
Before opening the electrical cabinet: always turn off the MAIN SWITCH and secure it against being switched back on again.
Wait for the discharge time to pass (at least 30 minutes).
Observe warning signs.

6.3 Thermal hazard

Hot workpieces

After the laser processing, the finished workpieces are hot. There is a risk of burning during removal.

Hot workpiece burns!

Allow workpieces to cool.
Wear protective gloves.
Use additional equipment to remove parts.

Hot surfaces

Components in the immediate vicinity of the processing point (e.g. clamping fixtures, nozzle) become hot during laser processing.

Risk of burns due to hot surfaces!

Wear protective gloves.

Components (e.g., motors) that get hot during operation are marked with a warning sign.

Risk of burns due to hot surfaces!

Do not touch hot surfaces.

Fire hazard

Firefighting must be performed only by personnel who are trained to do so and who are using appropriate protective equipment. If there is a risk of self-endangerment, or if the fire threatens to escalate, the fire department must be called.

Extinguishing agent: The following extinguishing agents should be kept ready for fire protection.

CO2 hand-held fire extinguisher (fire classification B, 5 kg or 11 lb) for fires in the compact dust extractor and for fires at the machine (except for metal fires).
Hand-held fire extinguisher (fire classification D, 12 kg or 26 lb) for metal fires.

Compact dust extractor: Sparks can be produced by the machining process. If the spark catcher is insufficiently or improperly maintained, sparks may enter the compact dust extractor. This can ignite the filter packs in the compact dust extractor.

Risk of injury from fire!

Clean the spark catcher regularly.

Processing of light metals: Light metals (e.g., aluminum, titanium) are highly reactive material. Fire may occur during laser processing.

Conventional extinguishing agents such as water, N2, CO2 promote combustion.

Risk of injury from fire!

Do not extinguish fires with water, N2 and CO2.
Keep a hand-held fire extinguisher ready for metal fires (fire class D).
Before cutting aluminum sheet, remove dust from all the filter elements of the compact dust extractor before cutting. After cutting, remove dust from all the filter elements of the compact dust extractor again before next cutting.

Oil residue on workpieces: Oil residue on workpieces, and especially oil spills, will vaporize during laser processing. The oil vapor becomes incrusted onto tube walls and filter elements of the compact dust extractor. If there is a high concentration of oil vapor and significant deposits, then there is a risk of fires and explosions due to flying sparks.

Risk of injury by fire or explosion!

Clean the workpieces before processing.
Remove oil residues on the machine regularly.

Oxygen is required for laser cutting. As a combustion-assisting gas, oxygen may cause explosion if it comes into contact with oil residues.

Risk of injury by fire or explosion!

Store combustible materials in a safe position.

Laser cutting of film-coated workpieces: Film lint can be formed during laser cutting of film-coated workpieces. The lint formation increases the risk of fire.

Risk of injury from fire!

If film lint occurs, cancel cutting process and change cutting parameters in coordination with JFY.

Combustible materials: Combustible materials inside the work area can ignite, enter the compact dust extractor through the Suction system and cause a fire there.

Risk of injury from fire!

Do not store flammable materials, such as cigarettes, gloves, paper or cardboard in the safety cabin.
Regularly and thoroughly service the compact dust extractor.
Replace the dust container when the specified filling level is reached.
Ensure that the seal on the upper edge of the container forms an airtight seal.
Use the original container from the manufacturer.

Combustible materials outside the machine: Combustible objects outside the machine can be ignited by flying sparks and cause a fire.

Risk of injury from fire!

Do not store combustible objects, such as cardboard boxes and plastic containers, within two meters of the machine.

Changing the materials: During a material change, a metal fire may occur underneath the machine and in the slag cart.

Risk of injury from fire!

Empty the slag cart before changing the material from aluminum/aluminum alloys to ferrous materials or vice versa.
Regardless of the materials processed: empty the slag cart on a daily basis.
Clean the slag cart insertion area below the machine extensively on a weekly basis.

Extinguishing fire on the machine

Means, Tools, Materials

CO2 hand-held fire extinguisher (fire classification B, 5 kg or 11 lb) for fires in the compact dust extractor and for fires at the machine (except for metal fires).
Hand-held fire extinguisher (fire classification D, 12 kg or 26 lb) for metal fires.

Risk of burns from flames and hot

component!

Wear protective gloves and safety glasses.
Only touch hot components with protective gloves.

1. Press the EMERGENCY STOP push-button.

2. Switch off the MAIN SWITCH of the machine.

3. Extinguish the fire from a safe distance with the correct hand-held fire extinguisher.

Extinguishing fire in the slag cart

During a material change, a metal fire may occur underneath the machine and in the slag cart.

Means, Tools, Materials

Hand-held fire extinguisher (fire classification D, 12 kg or 26 lb) for metal fires
Metal drum with dry sand (at least 20 L).

Risk of burns from flames and hot

Components!

Wear protective gloves and safety glasses.
Only touch hot components with protective gloves.

1. Press the EMERGENCY STOP push-button.

2. Switch off the MAIN SWITCH of the machine.

3. Completely pull out the slag cart.

4. From a distance of a few meters to the machine, extinguish the fire with sand or with the fire extinguisher for metal fires (fire classification D).

Compact dust extractor: extinguishing fire

A fire can start in the spark catcher, raw gas space or in the dust container.

After a fire in the compact dust extractor, the machine may only be put back into operation after the Technical Service from JFY or a company contracted by the Technical Service from JFY has repaired the compact dust extractor.

Means, Tools, Materials

CO2 hand-held fire extinguisher (fire classification B) for fires in the compact dust extractor.
Protective gloves.
Safety glasses.
Protective mask.

  1. If a fire occurs in the compact dust extractor (fumes from the clean gas outlet):

-Press the EMERGENCY STOP button.

-In case of separate power supply: Switch off MAIN SWITCH of the compact dust extractor.

2. Put on protective equipment.

6.4 Noise hazards

Noise pollution

Noise pollution!

Damage to hearing (deafness) or other

physiological impairments.

Wear hearing protection.

Proper ceiling installation will reduce noise damage to the operator’s hearing. However, in this case, it is still necessary to monitor the operator’s hearing status at all times. If the working environment changes, it is necessary to re-measure the noise level and take appropriate protective measures.

6.5 Hazard due to laser radiation

TruFiber beam source:The laser device generates invisible laser radiation.

Biological tissue absorbs light in different ways. It is usually converted into heat and causes thermal damage (e.g. burns, destruction of protein) and permanent retina damage.

Eyes and skin are especially at risk due to direct and reflective laser radiation.

Invisible, high-energy laser radiation!

Risk of severe skin burns and damage

to the eyes. Impairment or even loss of eyesight possible!

Only operate the machine with the guards active.
Check the guards for damage daily.
Observe warning signs and warning symbols.

Reflected laser radiation can escape if the slag carts are not fully inserted.

Reflected laser radiation!

Risk of severe skin burns and damage

to the eyes. Impairment or even loss of eyesight possible!

Insert slag carts completely.
Check rubber fin regularly for damage.

Damaged rubber fin: Scattered laser radiation may escape and cause damage to the eyes if the rubber fin above the slag cart is damaged.

Risk of injury due to laser radiation!

Laser radiation causes irreparable damage

to the eyes.

Do not look directly into the beam
Observe warning signs.
Observe the compulsory maintenance work requirements.

6.6 Hazards due to secondary radiation

Secondary radiation during laser processing

Secondary radiation develops during laser processing due to the interaction between the laser beam and material. The secondary radiation is composed of invisible ultraviolet (<400 nm) and infrared radiation (>780 nm) and harsh visible radiation.

Looking directly at the processing point can lead to blinding, corneal burns or damage to the retina.

Intensive dazzling of the eye leads to a temporary impairment of your vision and can leave an image in your eyes. This can lead to confusion, impairments or accidents.

Secondary radiation during laser

processing!

Irreversible eye damage.

Do not look at the processing point for a long time.
In case of visual contact: Wear darkened safety glasses with UV protection.

6.7 Danger due to materials and substances

Hazardous materials for laser processing

Laser processing of metals produces dust, aerosols and gases. Depending on particle size and concentration, these substances can be harmful to health.

In addition, the processing of alloyed steel (e. g. stainless steel), nickel-based alloys (e. g. Inconel) and galvanized sheet metal causes carcinogenic, mutagenic, toxic-to-reproduction or toxic substances. In these cases, hazardous material measurements must be initiated at the workstation and the necessary safety measures taken.

No carcinogenic, mutagenic, toxic to reproduction or toxic substances are produced during the processing of mild steel and aluminum alloys.

The user is obliged to comply with the national workplace limit values for all hazardous materials.

Health hazard due to inhaling dust,

aerosols and gases!！

Only operate the machine with suitable and functioning compact dust extractor.
The working environment should be well ventilated.
No processing with plastics.

Leaking cooling water

The cooling water contains additives (e.g. biocide and anti-corrosive agents), which could escape into the environment in the event of a leak in the cooling water circuit.

Aggressive cooling water additives in

cooling water!

Chemical burns on eyes, skin, mucous membranes and digestive tract.

Wear safety glasses and protective gloves.
Avoid direct contact with the eyes and skin.
Do not swallow cooling water.

6.8 Ergonomic hazard

Lifting heavy loads

Risk of injury due to heavy loads during loading and unloading the over-weighted sheets and cut parts.

Risk of injury when lifting heavy loads!

Use appropriate equipment and tools when lifting heavy loads,
Observe two-person operation if necessary.
Observe national regulation on labor protection.
