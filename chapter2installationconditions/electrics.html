Electrics

Target group

The specifications in the "Electrical" section must be implemented by a company which specializes in electrical installations.

IEC conditions/NEC conditions

IEC conditions apply worldwide. NEC conditions apply in USA and Canada only.

5.1 Power supply

Power supply should be well-grounded with a grounding resistance lower than 4Ω.

Machine power supply must use the three-phase five-wire system (L1、 L2、 L3、 N、 PE).

When maintenance work is carried out, main switch of the corresponding component must be switched off (laser device, dust extractor, process cooler).

NEC requirements for customer-provided electrical connection of the laser device

The laser device is powered by the machine. If the laser is to be connected to a household power supply, a main switch in compliance with UL489 must be installed before the connection point of the laser device.
For other information regarding specifications of the main switch, please refer the Operator’s Manual of the laser device.

Connected load

Impermissible voltage fluctuations endanger the faultless operation of the machines and reduce their performance. A voltage stabilizer is necessary.
The tolerance range applies to brief fluctuations. Measures to stabilize the mains must be taken in the event of continually non-permitted fluctuations. These measures must be coordinated with the energy supplier.
The machine contains highly dynamic components that have an effect on the connected load. The highly dynamic components require a dynamically responsive power supply system. A prerequisite for the power supply system is low impedance and sufficient short-circuit power. Standard values: Overload capacity for 0.5 s with 200 % of the connected load.
The standard values must be adhered to, particularly for power supply systems with low line voltage (e.g. 200 V or 208V). The occurring currents are here twice as high as for power supply systems with 400 V.
The measures and requirements must be agreed upon with the energy supplier or power supply outfitter.

NEC requirements of the fuse

Slow-blow fuse should be used preferentially. If the fuse is also used for branch circuit, an ETI fuse must be used to avoid peak current overloading during the machine start-up.

5.2 Power supply system

Leakage currents

Leakage currents greater than 10 mA can occur at the ground wire connection when power electronics are used and EMC protective measures are taken. A corresponding warning sign is present at the power connection.

Additional requirements for the electrical equipment regarding the leakage currents on the protective earth conductor (PE) must be taken into account by the user in accordance with locally applicable standards/regulations.

Residual current device （RCD）

Whether a residual current circuit breaker has to be used depends on the local electricity company.
On the line side, only residual current circuit breakers of type B (sensitive to all types of fault current, EN 50178/5.2.11.2; VDE 0160) are approved.

A fault current circuit breaker provides protection from fault currents due to directly or indirectly touching live parts.

Residual current circuit breaker for direct contact

If a fault current circuit breaker is used to provide protection from direct contact, an isolating transformer must be used (EN 50178 section 5.2.11.1, VDE 0160).

Residual current circuit breaker for indirect contact

If a fault current circuit breaker is to be used to provide protection from indirect contact, this must be selected depending on the machine’s fault current.

In rare cases, user-side voltage fluctuations on the machine’s power input filter may lead to higher leakage currents than the typically specified value. The main power supply system and the machine must then be separated by an isolating transformer. Typical leakage currents are 300 mA.

Power supply configurations

NEC conditions

Power supply configurations:

A Y power supply configuration with a grounded star point(Solidly Grounded Wye) is required.
In an existing delta power supply configuration(Corner Grounded Delta), a grounded isolating transformer in star configuration must be installed by the user, (see NEC article 450-5).

2. Installation method of the grounding device

The grounding rod must be inserted into the ground with sufficient depth. Before installing the artificial grounding rod, dig a hole about 1 meter deep. Drive the rod into the ground until the top end stands about 0.2m above the bottom of the pit for grounding wire connection. After the grounding device installation is completed, backfill the hole with earth. For rocky terrains, the grounding rod can be installed horizontally with a depth of no less than 0.6m. For improved reliability, at least two artificial grounding rods should be installed vertically at 2.5m-3m apart. Additionally, the top end of the rod should be connected to the flat or round steel in one piece.

The length of the rod below the ground should be painted to avoid high grounding resistance. All connection must be secured. Normally all components are welded together. For flat steel, the required length at connection point must be twice of its width. For round steel, the required length at connection point must be 6 times of it width.

Grounding wire and grounding rod are connected by means of welding or clamping for convenient inspection. Grounding wire and electric equipment can be connected by welding or bolt connection. In case of bolt connection, please tighten all the bolts to avoid loosening.
In case of two or more pieces of electrical equipment, grounding wires must be connected from the electrical equipment to the grounding device separately. Do not connect multiple pieces of electrical equipment in series and ground at the last electrical equipment. Otherwise, if the grounding wire of an electrical equipment is removed, all the equipment before this electrical equipment will no longer be grounded.
After the grounding system is installed, use a ground resistance meter to test the grounding resistance. The lower the value, the better the case. The CNC machines are low-voltage electrical equipment and their protective grounding resistance is 4Ω.
When the machine is in normal operation, regular inspection and test should be carried out on the grounding device at least once a year. After machine overhaul, the grounding wire connection of the natural grounding electrode should be checked for reliable contact and if there are any broken wires.

Over-voltage protection/Surge protection device（SPD）：

Machines are designed to incorporate safety circuits. Additional requirements for the electrical equipment regarding over voltage must be taken into account by the user in accordance with locally applicable standards/regulations.

Protective bonding and functional bonding

External normally live components (e.g. units) in the immediate vicinity of the machine must be connected to the machine with protective bonding.

External, conductive components in the immediate surroundings of the machine that are involved in the process (either through mechanical movements or through static charging by separation of charge), must be electrically connected to the machine for functional bonding.

External, conductive components (e.g. safety fences, handrails) in the immediate surroundings of the machine that do not have any live conductors, can be electrically connected to the machine for functional bonding.
