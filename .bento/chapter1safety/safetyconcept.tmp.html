Safety Chapter

The "Safety" chapter describes the safety concept of the machine. It specifies the guards and the manufacturer’s design measures for safe operation of the machine.

It points out dangers when operate the machine and specifies measures taken by the user to avoid possible dangers.

The "Hazards" section is based on the manufacturer’s risk assessment and describes the residual risk. The user must take measures to prevent the hazards. The measures must comply with the national safety and accident prevention regulations of the country in which the machine is operated.

Warning signs and information plates

Dangerous points on the machine are marked with warning signs and information plates.

Warnings

This operation manual contains warnings, which warn of dangers and specify measures to avoid these dangers.

Warnings have the following structure:

The signal word designates the degree of a danger.
The danger description specifies the danger and the possible consequences if attention is not paid to the danger.
The measures for prevention of danger are planned.

Examples of a warning: Risk of fatal injury due to falling load!

Observe the safety regulations for transporting heavy loads.
Never walk under a suspended load.
Use certified lifting power and means of transportation of sufficient size.
Employ qualified personnel to transport the machine.
Carry out transport in accordance with the transport regulations.

Warning notices include the following warnings:

Table 1-1

Signaling word Description

Danger

If the dangerous situation is not prevented, death or serious injuries will result.

Warning

If the dangerous situation is not prevented, death or serious injuries could result

Caution

If the dangerous situation is not prevented, light injuries could result.

Notice

If the situation is not prevented, damage to property could result.
