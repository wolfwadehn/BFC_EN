The arrangement of the components and required space for the installation of the system have been documented in the JFY installation plan.

3.2 Floor requirements

Surface

Permissible flatness tolerance:

Basic surface: Maximum 3mm/m2 .

Complete machine footprint range: Maximum 10mm.

Floor quality

Minimum curing time for concrete:

Regular concrete: 14 days.

High strength concrete: 7days.

If the machine is to be installed on the existing foundation of the shop, the thickness of the concrete must not be less 250mm.

Minimum compressive strength of the basic concrete: no less than 2000 tonnes/m2 after 28 days.

3.3 Stress due to vibration

In the immediate area around the system, external influences can lead to vibration loads. Vibration stress can affect the quality of workpieces.

The following requirements must be met: Acceleration< 0.03G; Amplitude < 3um.

External influences are as the followings:

Forklifts, industrial trucks, etc.
Installation or removal of other machines in the immediate vicinity of the system.
Machines which generate vibrations during operation, such as punching presses etc.

3.4 Ambient conditions

Ambient conditions

One-sided exposure to direct sunlight and unidirectional drafts must be avoided (e.g. with Venetian blinds if installed near a window).
The ambient temperature should be kept constant while operating the system. Sufficient ventilation should be ensured in the shop.

3.5 Exhaust air system of the compact dust extractor

The exhaust air system beginning at the clear gas duct of the compact dust extractor must be installed by the customer.

International/national regulation

Regulations in regions out of Germany：

During laser processing, carcinogenic, mutagenic or fertility-endangering airborne dusts may be generated if the processed materials are alloyed with chromium and nickel, for example. Depending on the national regulations, the exhaust air of the compact dust extractor may have to be led into the open air.

Regulations in Germany：

During the laser processing of stainless steel and other materials, airborne dusts harmful to health may be generated. The exhaust air has to be led into the open air.

  • In Germany, “Technical Regulations for Hazardous Substances TRGS 560” specifies the limited value for the cleaned air recirculated into the room air.

    For the laser processing of mild steel, galvanized mild steel, aluminum, copper, brass or titanium for example, the cleaned air of the compact dust extractor is not allowed to feed back into the room air.

Exhaust air system

The exhaust air system must be installed as follows:

Max. of two 90° tube bends (radius = diameter x 1.5).
Max. Tube diameter：250mm
Max. length from the dust extractor blower to the ventilation outlet: 3m
In case that only the blower is available for use, the blower is recommended to be installed outdoors. Air duct should be designed in accordance with the geometry of the air outlet of the blower. If a dust extraction and purification system is in use, the cleaned air may be fed back into the shop.

For other configurations, a ventilation technician or the manufacturer of the compact dust extractor must be consulted. The max. resistance of 100 Pa must not be exceeded.

3.6 Cable rack for laser light cables

The laser light cable is installed by the Technical Service.

If the laser device is not set up according to the standard floor layout, the following applies:

The laser light cable is guided in cable racks from the installation site of the laser to the machine. The user must install the cable racks before the machine is delivered.
The cable racks are to be constructed as follows:
{blank}

  • 250 mm (10 in) Width of the cable racks 250 mm (10 in).

  • Use cable racks open on one side made of slats; thus the cable will be able to be inserted and fastened in place easily from the side during installation.

  • Design the corner elements and transitions in such a way that a minimum radius of 200 mm (8 in) is always ensured.

    The distance between the LLK and the low voltage cables must be at least 50 mm (2 in).
    The LLK is not permitted to be laid together with a high voltage cable in the same cable rack.
    If cable racks are used without slats, then the LLK must be fastened in place with cable binders.
    The LLK must not be pulled, pushed, impacted or turned.
    Wall openings must have a minimum diameter of 100 mm (4 in) and be designed with smooth surfaces (e.g. with a core drill).。

3.7 Installation of the process cooler

Outdoor installation

If the process cooler is installed outdoors, the following must be observed:

Process coolers for outdoor installation have their own main switch and require a separate power supply.
Process coolers for outdoor installation have tank heating.
At ambient temperatures below 0 ℃, the process cooler's MAIN SWITCH must not be switched off.
At ambient temperatures of < +6 ℃, ethylene glycol must be added to the cooling water.
During the installation of the process cooler, make sure no escaping water or oil pass into the ground.
A weather protection cover, or weather protection hood is required for the process cooler. In order to allow access for maintenance and repair work, comply with the minimum distances as shown in the drawing.
Figure 1. Figure 2- 1

Platform installation

If process coolers malfunction, water or oil can escape from the device. If process coolers are placed on a pedestal, the area underneath the pedestal is at risk.

Escaping cooling water or oil!

Machines, devices, or components underneath the pedestal can be damaged or destroyed.

The platform must be designed in such a way that no escaping water or oil passes can enter the area below the platform.

The clearance between the top of the process cooler and the ceiling of the factory hall must be at least 1500 mm (60 in) to ensure sufficient circulation of the warm exhaust air of the process cooler.
