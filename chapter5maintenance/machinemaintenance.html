Machine maintenance

Machine maintenance

Maintenance is performed by the operator if the maintenance frequency is within 2 weeks, otherwise performed by the people have been trained.

2.1 Machine lubrication

Lubrication is primarily targeted for the transmission part of the machine. Z-axis guide rail and ball screw are lubricated by self-lubrication mechanism. The pinion and rack systems should be lubricated by central lubrication once a week, the grease type is 0# extreme pressure lithium base grease. The chain of the machine should be lubricated with grease once a month by people, the grease type is 46# grease.

2.2 The chain of the machine

The chain of the machine should be inspected every 6 months. Re-tighten the chain if it’s found to be loose. Tightening procedures: Loosen the screws on the installation plate of the chain wheel. Adjust the chain wheel to a proper position with the adjustment screw of the chain wheel. Tighten the nuts on the installation plate of the chain wheel after adjustment.

2.3 Inspecting the cooling water circuit

Make sure that the distilled water is used for cooling water of the process chillers. Otherwise, the service life of the laser device and lens will be affected. Replace the water every two months. Otherwise, the flow rate will be affected by the change in water quality. Outlet pressure of the cooling water should be set to 0.4Mpa ~ 0.5Mpa. Cooling water temperature of the external light path is normally set to 28℃. Cooling water temperature of the laser device is normally set to 26℃ (in winter) and 26℃ (in summer). Fluctuation range of the water temperature is ±1℃. In case of a high cooling water temperature in the external light path, setting temperature of cooling water should be increased appropriately to prevent condensation on the lens, which will affect the service life of the lens.

Figure 2. Figure 5- 2
Before switching on the process cooler, check the cooling water level. The process cooler has an automatic protection function when the water level is too low. In this case, please add distill water before switching on the machine.
Check if the supply valve and return valve are open before switching on the machine to prevent the burst of the water pipes due to high pressure.
If the ambient temperature is below 0 ℃ and the machine is not operated for over 24 hours. Empty the water tank to prevent water freezing in the tank or piping and damage to the laser device or process coolers.
Do not leave the side cover of the process cooler for a long time. This may affect the cooling effect.
Clean the filter on the side of the process cooler every two week to maintain the cooling effect.
Cooling water and filter element should be replaced every two month. Otherwise, the flow rate will be affected by the change in water quality.

2.4 Draining the process cooler and laser device

In areas with a long-term subfreezing temperature, if the machine is not operated (e.g. long holidays) for a long period, empty all the water in the process cooler and laser device to avoid frozen internal water circuit.

Follow the steps below to empty the water in process cooler and laser device:

Open the valve on water inlet “IN” and water outlet “OUT” and allow the water to flow freely.
When the water flow stops, connect compressed air to water inlet “IN”. At the same time, the water outlet “OUT” should remain opened.
Increase the air pressure from 0.1MPa to 0.2MPa slowly. Keep blowing for 5 minutes. Notes: Do no increase the air pressure sharply.
Increase the air pressure from 0.3MPa to 0.4MPa slowly. Keep blowing for 10 minutes. Notes: Do no increase the air pressure sharply.
Connect compressed air to water outlet “OUT” and open water inlet “IN”. Repeat step 3 and 4 as mentioned above.
After emptying the water, connect the plugs to water inlet “IN” and water outlet “OUT”.

2.5 Removing dust from electrical cabinet

Due to the large amount of metal dust generated during laser processing, it’s recommended to remove the dust in the electrical cabinet of the laser cutting machine regularly (once a month).The working condition of cooling fan should also be checked to avoid problems caused by dust buildup such as overheating, short circuit, malfunction and premature failure of the components etc.

Follow the instructions below when removing the dust from the electrical cabinet:

Clean the cooling fan and dust filter mat of the electrical cabinet regularly.
Use a rechargeable vacuum clean with a small intake port suitable for removing dust from electrical components and electrical racks.
Blow off the dust with a soft bristle and air box.
Get rid of the dust while it’s still easy to do so rather than wait until the dust builds up. Keep the electrical cabinet clean to avoid the malfunctions.

Note：

Please turn off the power before opening the electrical cabinet.
